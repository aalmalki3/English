🌟 Unit 1 · All about you

In this unit: verb be with I/you/we; hello & goodbye; names & alphabet; numbers 0-10; asking How about you?; everyday expressions.

Lesson A: Hello and good‑bye

🎧 Matt: Good morning, Sarah. How are you?
Sarah: Good. How are you, Matt?
Matt: I'm fine, thanks.
Matt: Hello. I'm Matt Lenski.
Emily: Hi, I'm Emily Kim. Nice to meet you.
Matt: Nice to meet you.
▶ B. Complete these conversations. Use your own names.
A: Hello. I'm ________.    B: Hi. Nice to meet you. ________.    A: Nice to meet ________.
🎧 Building vocabulary – responses: Bye / Have a good evening / See you tomorrow / You too.
✅ Write three expressions for hello and goodbye. Then say hello to five classmates.

Lesson B: Saying names & alphabet

A: What's your name?   B: Catherine Ravelli.
A: How do you spell Catherine?   B: C-A-T-H-E-R-I-N-E.   A: Thanks. And your last name?   B: R-A-V-E-L-L-I.
📖 Verb be (I, you, we)
I'm Jenny / I'm not Carmen  |  You're in Room G  |  We're in the same class.
Questions: Are you Jenny? Yes, I am. / No, I'm not. Are we late? Yes, we are.
✏️ Complete the conversation:
A: _____ you Emiko? B: Yes, I _____. _____ you here for English? A: No, I _____. I'm here for French.

Lesson C: Personal information & numbers 0–10

Numbers: zero, one, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten.

Receptionist: What's your phone number? Victor: It's 646-555-3048.
Receptionist: And your e‑mail? Victor: vlopez6@cup.org.
🔹 What's your name? → My name's Victor.   What's your phone number? → It's 646-555-3048.
📋 Match questions & answers:
1. What's your teacher's name? ___   a. 646-555-3907   b. Ms. Gardino   c. yoyo3@cup.org

Lesson D: Conversation strategy – How about you?

💬 How about you? – Adam asks the same question back: "Are you here for the concert? Yeah. How about you?"
✅ Practice: Lora: Are you new here? Ying: Yes, I am. ______ ? Lora: Yes, me too.
✨ Everyday expressions: Yes ↔ Yeah ; Thank you ↔ Thanks ; Hello ↔ Hi ; I'm fine ↔ Pretty good / OK.
💬 Free talk: Meet a celebrity – introduce yourself and complete name cards for three celebrities.

📚 Unit 2 · In class

In this unit: verb be (he/she/they); classroom items; this/these; where questions; asking for help; responses to Thank you / I'm sorry.

Lesson A: Where's Jun?

Miss Cass: Where's Jun? Is he here today? Ana: No, he's not. Maybe he's at work.
Miss Cass: How about Laura? Ana: I think she's sick. Miss Cass: Are Kim and Phong here? Ana: No, they're in the cafeteria.
✔️ He's / She's / They're
Jun is at work → He's at work. Laura is sick → She's sick. Kim and Phong are late → They're late.
Negative: He's not here. / They're not in class.
📝 Complete: "Is David sick? No, he _____. He _____ in class." (is / is not)

Lesson B: What's in your bag?

a / an: a pen, an eraser, a dictionary, an MP3 player, a watch, glasses, keys, sunglasses.

🔹 This / These & noun plurals
What's this? → It's an MP3 player.   What are these? → They're sunglasses.
Is this your watch? Yes, it is.   Are these your keys? No, they're not.
Plurals: bag→bags, watch→watches, dictionary→dictionaries.
✏️ Ask and answer: "Is this your ______?" / "Are these your ______?" Use pictures.

Lesson C: In the classroom & where questions

Mrs. Evans: Where's the VCR? Paula: It's in the closet. Mrs. Evans: Where are the students' homework papers? Paula: They're on the floor.
🗺️ Where's / Where are; possessives 's and s'
Where's Mario's homework? → It's on the floor.   Where are the students' papers? → They're on the desk.
teacher's coat / three students' answers.
🔍 Pair work: Ask "Where's the teacher's desk?" "Where's the clock?" etc.

Lesson D: Asking for help in class

❓ Common questions: "What's the word for this in English?" "How do you spell 'book'?" "Can I borrow your pen, please?"
Responses: "Sure. Here you go." – "Thank you." → "You're welcome." – "I'm sorry." → "That's OK."
👂 Listening & following instructions: Turn to page 9, answer the questions, write the word "eraser" in your notebook.

⭐ Unit 3 · Favorite people

possessive adjectives; be in info questions; describing personality; family; showing interest with Really?

Lesson A: Celebrities

Sandra: Sean Penn – his new movie is great. Norah Jones – her voice is amazing. The Williams sisters – their matches are exciting.
📌 Possessive adjectives: my, your, his, her, our, their
I'm a fan → My favorite actor. He's an actor → His movie. They're players → Their matches.
✏️ Circle correct: "I'm / My a Sting fan. He's / His music is amazing."

Lesson B: People we know (personality adjectives)

smart, shy, friendly, outgoing, strict, lazy, interesting, fun, tired, busy.

Dana: What are your co‑workers like? Tim: They're really friendly. Dana: Is your boss strict? Tim: No, she isn't very strict.

Lesson C: Family & numbers 10–101

Family words: grandmother/grandpa, mother/father, wife/husband, daughter/son, sister/brother, aunt/uncle, cousin.

Numbers: 10 ten, 11 eleven, 15 fifteen, 20 twenty, 30 thirty, 40 forty, 50 fifty, 100 one hundred, 101 a hundred and one.

❓ Information questions with be
How old is your father? Where are your parents from? Who's that? What's she like? How are your grandparents?
🧩 Unscramble: "are / from / parents / originally / where / your ?" → ___________________.

Lesson D: Showing interest – Really?

🗣️ Show interest by repeating and asking: "She's from London." → "London? Wow. Is she a student?"
✨ Really? is a top 50 word: "My best friend is a singer." → "Really? Is she in a band?"
🎤 Pair work: Tell about a friend; partner shows interest: "Really? What's he like?"
👪 Vocabulary notebook – All in the family (make your own family tree, write ages).

🌅 Unit 4 · Everyday life

simple present statements & yes‑no questions; daily/weekly routines; answering more than yes/no; using Well.

Lesson A: In the morning

Greg: I get up early. I check my e‑mail and listen to the radio. Jennifer: I don't like mornings. My sister watches TV, my brother plays computer games.
Amanda: We get up late, so I eat breakfast in the car. My husband doesn't have breakfast.
⏰ Simple present statements
I eat breakfast. / I don't eat lunch.   He listens to the radio. / She doesn't watch videos.
Verb endings: get→gets, watch→watches, study→studies, have→has, do→does.
✏️ Complete: "My mother ______ (not / watch) TV. My parents ______ (talk) a lot."

Lesson B: Routines – Do you …?

Activities: do homework, play sports, clean the house, go shopping, do the laundry, make phone calls, take lessons, check e‑mail.

❓ Yes/No questions & short answers
Do you go to a class in the evening? Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
Does your mother work on weekends? Yes, she does. / No, she doesn't.
📋 Survey: "Find someone who belongs to a club / plays on a team / has breakfast in the car."

Lesson C: Saying more than yes or no (and Well)

💬 Friendly answers: "Do you live around here?" → "No, I live near the beach."
"Are you a student?" → "Yes, I'm a law student."
Use Well to think: "Well, I'm from Chicago originally." / "Well, not really."
Tina: Do you come here every day? Ray: No... Well, I have breakfast here before class.
🗣️ Pair work: Answer "Do you have a part‑time job?" with more than yes/no. Use Well if needed.

Lesson D: Reading & writing – Average American

Reading: In a lifetime, an American works 90,000 hours, sleeps 24 years, watches TV for 12 years, spends 2.5 years on the phone.

✍️ Writing task: Write an e‑mail about your partner's average week. Use capital letters and periods.
Example: "On average, I study 10 hours a week. I use my cell phone 5 times a day."
🖍️ Vocabulary notebook: Verbs, verbs, verbs – draw simple pictures: read the newspaper, exercise, watch TV, do homework.
