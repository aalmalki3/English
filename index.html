Welcome to Touchstone![cite: 1]

We created the Touchstone series with the help of the Cambridge International Corpus of North American English.[cite: 1] The corpus is a large database of language from everyday conversations, radio and television broadcasts, and newspapers and books.[cite: 1]

Using computer software, we analyze the corpus to find out how people actually use English.[cite: 1] We use the corpus as a "touchstone" to make sure that each lesson teaches you authentic and useful language.[cite: 1] The corpus helps us choose and explain the grammar, vocabulary, and conversation strategies you need to communicate successfully in English.[cite: 1]

Touchstone makes learning English fun.[cite: 1] It gives you many different opportunities to interact with your classmates.[cite: 1] You can exchange personal information, take class surveys, role-play situations, play games, and discuss topics of personal interest.[cite: 1] Using Touchstone, you can develop confidence in your ability to understand real-life English and to express yourself clearly and effectively in everyday situations.[cite: 1]

We hope you enjoy using Touchstone and wish you every success with your English classes.[cite: 1]

Michael McCarthy[cite: 1]
Jeanne McCarten[cite: 1]
Helen Sandiford[cite: 1]

Unit features[cite: 1]

  • Getting started presents new grammar in natural contexts such as surveys, interviews, conversations, and phone messages.[cite: 1]
  • Figure it out challenges you to notice how grammar works.[cite: 1]
  • Building language builds on the grammar presented in Lesson A.[cite: 1]
  • In conversation panels tell you about the grammar and vocabulary that are most frequent in spoken North American English.[cite: 1]
  • Grammar is presented in clear charts.[cite: 1]
  • Grammar exercises give you practice with new structures and opportunities to exchange personal information with your classmates.[cite: 1]
  • Speaking naturally helps you understand and use natural pronunciation and intonation.[cite: 1]
  • Building vocabulary uses pictures to introduce new words and expressions.[cite: 1]
  • Word sort helps you organize vocabulary and then use it to interact with your classmates.[cite: 1]
  • Talk about it encourages you to discuss interesting questions with your classmates.[cite: 1]
  • Conversation strategy helps you "manage" conversations better.[cite: 1]
  • Listening and speaking skills are often practiced together.[cite: 1]
  • Free talk helps you engage in free conversation with your classmates.[cite: 1]
